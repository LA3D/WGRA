PREFIX rdf: <http://www.w3.org/1999/02/22-rdf-syntax-ns#>

SELECT ?topicA ?topicB (COUNT(*) AS ?crossrefs) WHERE {
    ?paperA llm-wiki-colab:related ?paperB ;

What the graph reveals

Five observations that fall out of the graph rather than the prose:

  1. Even coverage. Papers per topic sits in a narrow 5–9 band across 16 named topics. The survey is not lopsided toward one flagship area at the expense of others, which is the health signal a literature survey wants to see.
  2. Foundation models sit at the chemistry–autonomous-labs junction. The two strongest cross-topic bonds are Chemistry ↔ Foundation Models (18) and Autonomous Labs ↔ Foundation Models (14), consistent with the self-driving-chemistry story where foundation-model-driven agents run wet-lab work. Foundation Models is doing structural work as the connective tissue between two adjacent domains, not just as its own topic.
  3. Engineering underweighted, and Mechanical Engineering is isolated. Natural-science topics average 8+ papers per topic; engineering disciplines average 5–6. But the sharper finding is that AI-for-Mechanical-Engineering has zero cross-topic related: edges to any other topic. Its six papers connect only among themselves. The other engineering topics (Civil, Electrical, Aerospace) each have at least some cross-refs. Mechanical is the one topic actually isolated from the wiki's interdisciplinary conversation. That is the concrete next-ingest-pass signal.
  4. AI-Co-Scientist-Google-2026 is the crossroads paper. Flat inbound-degree makes it look identical to Coscientist-2023 and Robin-Multi-Agent-Discovery-2025, but bridge-count separates them (6 vs 3 vs 4). It is doing more interdisciplinary work than the other two landmarks combined and should be the first read for an agent orienting to the field.
  5. Body-mention structure agrees with frontmatter-typed structure. The top ten most-mentioned pages in prose bodies (via mentions edges) overlap heavily with the top ten by typed-edge inbound count: Foundation Models, Chemistry, Meta-Analyses, and the biology topics appear in both. When the prose and the frontmatter tell the same story about what matters, that is evidence the wiki-authoring discipline is being maintained. The frontmatter is not aspirational metadata detached from what the pages actually say.

The wiki has almost no extends: or criticizes: edges (one and zero respectively). That is characteristic of a survey wiki whose job is to catalog what exists rather than argue about it. It's not a fault; it's the intended shape at this project stage. A future phase — say, comparing methods across the papers — would introduce criticizes: edges as claims about which approaches fail on which benchmarks. The graph structure would immediately reflect that shift.
